Welcome to Spatial Reasoning!

Spatial reasoning skills are strongly correlated with success in engineering and computer science, especially at the introductory levels. Sometimes this is due to the spatial nature of the tasks, but more often it is because professors, texts, and peers use diagrams, figures, and spatial terms and ideas to help explain complicated but not intrinsically spatial concepts. Thus, many students, who didnâ€™t develop these skills before coming to college, are at a disadvantage.

This is a student-taught class, geared towards students who are interested in computer science or engineering, or thinking of taking a class on a related subject during their time at UVa.

Spatial reasoning skills have been shown to be strongly correlated with success in computer science, especially in the introductory levels. This is because, in many instances, the approach used in teaching computer science concepts involves the use of spatial terms and concepts.

Being comfortable with these terms in advance (or even learning them in more depth while taking a class that uses them) has been proved to be of great help to students.

What we will learn...

We have designed this class to be just the right mix of instructive and entertaining. We will meet once a week for one hour, to talk about Spatial Reasoning topics and engage in hands-on activities to enrich our learning.

We will cover topics such as: sketching; object rotations, reflections and symmetry; cross sections of solids; solid combinations; spatial relations; and spatial orientation.

What we will do...

The best way to learn is to do, so in order to enrich the learning experience, we have prepared several engaging hands-on activities to help students better understand the material. The activities we introduce in class have a connection to spatial reasoning concepts, and have been shown to drastically improve spatial skills.

These activities will include: drawing and sketching; modeling; creating mazes; and even playing video-games!
